# Roles and Responsibilities - Approval Scheme Process

This document defines the main organizational roles involved in operating the Approval Scheme Process and clarifies their responsibilities.

[Turkce surum](roles-and-responsibilities.tr.md)

## 1. Purpose

The model relies on clear accountability. Institutions should define who requests access, who approves it, who configures the control model, and who reviews outcomes after the fact.

## 2. Core Roles

### Employee

Responsibilities:

- Perform queries within assigned session context
- Provide justification for out-of-context requests
- Follow policy and avoid unnecessary access
- Cooperate with reviews and investigations

Must not:

- Approve their own requests
- Circumvent the approval process
- Use another person's credentials

### Supervisor

Responsibilities:

- Review low- and medium-risk requests within business context
- Confirm operational necessity
- Deny requests lacking sufficient justification
- Escalate requests when higher review is needed

Must not:

- Approve requests with unresolved conflict of interest
- Act outside delegated authority

### Security Officer

Responsibilities:

- Review requests with elevated security implications
- Validate that the requested access fits institutional security policy
- Participate in security-level and approval-scheme reviews

### Legal Reviewer

Responsibilities:

- Evaluate requests with legal implications
- Confirm legal basis for sensitive access where required
- Support policy definition for restricted categories of data

### Data Protection or Compliance Officer

Responsibilities:

- Review requests involving protected or sensitive personal data
- Assess data minimization and purpose alignment
- Support compliance reporting and investigations

### Senior Authorizing Authority

Responsibilities:

- Approve exceptional or critical access where policy requires senior oversight
- Review emergency or override cases that exceed normal approval chains

### System Administrator

Responsibilities:

- Implement approved configuration changes
- Maintain technical integrity of the platform
- Support logging, retention, and operational controls

Must not:

- Change approval policies without authorized governance approval
- Use administrative access to bypass normal business approval rules except under explicit emergency procedure

### Auditor or Oversight Reviewer

Responsibilities:

- Review logs, exceptions, denials, and unusual access patterns
- Confirm that reviews occurred on schedule
- Report control gaps and misuse indicators

## 3. RACI-Oriented Responsibility Map

The following model can be adapted by institutions:

- Request creation: Employee is responsible
- Business necessity review: Supervisor is accountable
- Security review: Security Officer is accountable where required
- Legal review: Legal Reviewer is accountable where required
- Data protection review: Compliance Officer is accountable where required
- Configuration implementation: System Administrator is responsible after approval
- Independent review: Auditor or Oversight Reviewer is accountable

## 4. Conflict of Interest Rules

Institutions should define at least these rules:

- A requester cannot approve their own request
- A direct beneficiary of access should not be the final approver
- A policy author should not be the sole approver for exceptions created under that policy
- Emergency access should always trigger independent post-review

## 5. Minimum Competency Expectations

Each role should receive training appropriate to its responsibilities.

Examples:

- Employees: acceptable use and justification quality
- Supervisors: business necessity review and misuse indicators
- Security staff: risk review and escalation handling
- Legal and compliance staff: regulated data handling
- Auditors: evidence review and exception tracing

## 6. Summary

The Approval Scheme Process depends on clearly separated responsibilities. Strong role definition reduces misuse, prevents control bypass, and improves auditability.
